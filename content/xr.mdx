# XR: Extended Reality

<Section id='what-is-xr'>
  ## What is Extended Reality (XR)?

**Extended Reality, or XR, is an umbrella term for technologies that integrate digital content with the real world.** XR consists of three elements: Augmented Reality (AR), Mixed Reality (MR), and Virtual Reality (VR).

We work across all three to create immersive, story-led experiences — from smartphone-based overlays to fully virtual environments — helping our clients engage audiences in new and memorable ways.

</Section>

<Section id='what-is-ar'>
  ## Augmented Reality (AR)

**AR overlays digital elements onto the real world.** Viewed through devices like smartphones or tablets, it enhances your environment by adding digital layers without replacing it.

We use AR to bring places, stories, and data to life in context — adding interactive layers to streetscapes, buildings, and public spaces. See [BT Urban.AR](/work/bt-urban-ar) for an example of AR applied to smart cities.

</Section>

<Section id='what-is-mr'>
  ## Mixed Reality (MR)

**MR uses a headset to anchor digital content in your real space.** You see and interact with virtual objects around you at real-world scale, blending physical and digital elements. MR is generally hands-free with no need for controllers.

We apply MR to fields like construction, heritage, and education — where seeing digital content at real-world scale changes how people understand and work with physical environments. Have a look at [Pop.XR](/work/pop-xr) for our phygital work and [Construct.AR](/work/construct-ar) for construction sector research.

</Section>

<Section id='what-is-vr'>
  ## Virtual Reality (VR)

**VR immerses you fully in a digital environment via a headset.** The physical world is completely replaced with a virtual one, giving creators total control over what the audience sees and feels.

We use VR to build powerful narrative experiences — placing audiences inside stories rather than in front of them. See [Window to the Soul](/work/window-to-the-soul) for an example.

</Section>
